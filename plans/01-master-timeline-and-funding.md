- At Round 1 (₹5-8Cr valuation): Their 5% = ₹25-40L → **1-1.6x in 6 months**
- At Round 2 (₹15-25Cr): Their 5% = ₹75L-1.25Cr → **3-5x in 18 months**
- At acquisition: Their 5% = ₹5-10Cr → **20-40x in 3-4 years**

**For Round 1 Investor (₹25L at 3-5%):**

- At acquisition: ₹3-10Cr → **12-40x in 2-3 years**

**Acquisition Targets (who would buy us):**
- ITC Foods, Tata Consumer, Marico, Dabur, Hindustan Unilever
- These companies are actively acquiring D2C food brands at 3-5x revenue
- A ₹50Cr revenue brand = ₹150-250Cr acquisition value

## INVESTOR PITCH SUMMARY (1-Page)

**Mom Ka Nuska** — A mother's authentic recipes, from a real farm, to your kitchen.

**Problem:** ₹25,000Cr Indian pickle/papad market dominated by factory-made products. Urban consumers crave "ghar jaisa" taste but have no trusted source.

**Solution:** Content-driven D2C food brand where a real mother (Anjula Singh) makes products on a real farm. Daily "Farm Diary" content builds trust and reduces CAC over time.

**Why Now:** D2C food brands proven (FarmDidi ₹18Cr ARR, Jhaji ₹9Cr+). Quick-commerce enables national reach without retail. Content-first brands win in 2026.

**Ask:** ₹25L for 5% (₹5Cr valuation)

**Use of Funds:** ₹12L farm setup + ₹8L initial inventory/ads + ₹5L working capital

**12-Month Target:** ₹1Cr revenue (₹50L B2B + ₹50L B2C), 1600 monthly orders, 25K+ social following

**Comparable Exits:** Veeba (acquired ₹750Cr), Raw Pressery (Tata acquired), Yoga Bar (ITC acquired ₹300Cr)
