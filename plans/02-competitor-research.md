## 1. Jhaji Store (jhajistore.com)

### The Story
- **Founded:** June 2021 by sisters-in-law **Kalpana Jha** (homemaker) & **Uma Jha** (school teacher) from Darbhanga, Bihar
- **Initial Investment:** ₹10 lakh (from Kalpana's husband Manoj Kumar Jha)
- **Origin:** Started making traditional Mithilanchal sun-dried pickles from their home backyard
- **USP:** Authentic Bihari recipes from Mithilanchal region, zero preservatives, sun-dried process

### Revenue Journey

| Period | Revenue | Key Event |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| FY 2021-22 | ~₹80 lakh | First full year, organic growth |
| Shark Tank S1 | No deal | But national TV exposure → sales tripled overnight |
| FY 2022-23 | ₹4+ crore | Post-Shark Tank surge, 5x growth |
| Post-ST Funding | ₹85 lakh | From Namita Thapar + Vineeta Singh + Jharkhand Angels |
| By July 2024 | ₹9+ crore cumulative | 200K orders, 150K families served |

### Product Strategy
- **38 total SKUs** across: Spicy (10), Mango (6), Mixed (5), Bihari specialty (8), Sweet (4), Kheer Mixes (3)
- **Hero Products:** Lal Mirch Bharua Achar (1245 reviews), Oal Pickle (568 reviews), 5-Pickle Sample Pack (1058 reviews)
- **Pricing:** ₹199-₹599 range, Sample packs at entry price, 15-23% combo discounts
- **Smart Move:** Sample packs as gateway → full-size conversion

### Operations
- Started home kitchen → dedicated kitchen factory in Darbhanga
- Warehouses in Patna, Gurgaon, Bengaluru (30% logistics cost reduction)
- 120+ employees (majority women)
- Ingredients sourced from local farmers (Samastipur, Muzaffarpur)

### Key Learnings for MKN
1. **Shark Tank effect is real** — even without a deal, visibility = sales
2. **Regional identity wins** — "Mithilanchal" positioning creates authenticity moat
3. **Sample packs are critical** — low barrier to trial, high conversion to full-size
4. **₹10L start → ₹4Cr in 2 years** is achievable with the right story + product
5. **Nanad-Bhabhi (sister-in-law duo) story** = powerful emotional narrative

## 2. FarmDidi (farmdidi.com)

### The Story
- **Founded:** 2021 by **Manjari Sharma**, **Anukrit Johari**, **Asmita Ghodeshwar**
- **Mission:** Empower 1 million rural women through homemade food production
- **Model:** Partner with SHGs (Self-Help Groups) → train rural women ("Didis") → they produce pickles from micro-units near their homes
- **USP:** "Tech-led Lijjat Papad" — tech-enabled quality control + batch-level traceability

### Revenue & Funding

| Milestone | Amount | Details |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Shark Tank S3 E45 | ₹1 Cr for 10% equity | From Vineeta Singh + Peyush Bansal |
| Asked valuation | ₹25 Cr | Got ₹10 Cr valuation |
| ARR (Sep 2025) | ~₹18 Cr | 30,000+ monthly orders |
| Seed Round (Sep 2025) | ₹7+ Cr | Led by Samved Ventures, LetsVenture, IIM Calcutta Innovation Park |

### Product Strategy
- **Focus:** Pickles, Chutneys, Masalas — all homemade, preservative-free
- **Massive variety:** 30+ pickle types organized by ingredient (Mango, Lemon, Chilli, Mixed, Garlic, Amla, etc.)
- **Regional recipes:** Punjabi, Rajasthani, Andhra styles
- **Pricing:** ₹199-₹499 per jar
- **4 Lakh+ customer base**

### Distribution
- Own website (Shopify)
- Amazon (top-selling pickle brand recognition)
- Quick-commerce: Blinkit, Zepto, BigBasket, Swiggy Instamart
- Pan-India delivery

### Key Learnings for MKN
1. **Social impact story = funding magnet** — investors love "empower women" narrative
2. **₹18Cr ARR with pickles alone** proves the category has massive headroom
3. **Quick-commerce is a must** — Blinkit/Zepto for metro penetration
4. **Tech traceability** (scan QR → see who made your pickle) builds trust
5. **Their ₹10Cr valuation at Shark Tank** → validates our ₹5Cr ask is reasonable

## 3. FirstBud Organics (firstbud.in)

### The Story
- **Founded:** 2017 (incorporated) by **Mitresh Sharma** & mother **Saroj Devi Sharma** from Deoghar, Jharkhand
- **Model:** Mother-son duo sourcing organic produce directly from farmers, bypassing middlemen
- **USP:** Pahadi/organic products — Lakadong Turmeric, Pahadi Honey, Herbal Teas, Pickles

### Revenue & Funding

| Period | Revenue | Details |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Previous FY | ~₹80 lakh | Bootstrapped growth |
| FY 2023-24 (to Nov) | ~₹1.5 Cr | Growing momentum |
| Shark Tank S3 E48 | ₹50L for 5% | From Ritesh Agarwal (OYO) |
| Valuation | ₹10 Cr | At deal time |
| Pre-ST | Completely bootstrapped | Reinvested all earnings |

### Product Strategy
- **~20 SKUs** across: Herbal Teas, Homemade Pickles, Honey & A2 Ghee, Immunity Drops, Spices, Sugar Substitutes
- **Hero Products:** Pahadi Honey (₹499/500g), Lakadong Turmeric (₹320), Mango Pickle (₹449), Detox Tea Combo (₹640)
- **Premium positioning:** A2 Gir Cow Ghee at ₹799-₹5,500
- **Sales channels:** Amazon (major), own Shopify site, CRED

### Key Learnings for MKN
1. **Mother-son duo = EXACTLY our model** — Vibhor + Anjula = same playbook
2. **₹80L → ₹1.5Cr bootstrapped** before any funding — proves organic growth works
3. **₹10Cr valuation with ₹1.5Cr revenue** = ~6.7x revenue multiple (validates our ₹5Cr at early stage)
4. **"Pahadi" origin story** creates premium perception — we need our regional origin story too
5. **A2 Ghee as high-AOV product** — consider similar premium SKUs

## Content Inspiration: @pahadansteam

### What They Do Right
- **Authentic Pahadi food content** — real kitchens, real ingredients, chulha cooking
- **Cultural storytelling** — not just recipes, but stories behind dishes
- **Fast-paced Reels** — hook in 3 seconds, dynamic cuts, hero shot at end
- **Regional music** — Pahadi folk music in background creates identity
- **Educational value** — explain unique ingredients (Jakhiya, Gahat, Bhatt)

### How MKN Should Adapt This
1. **"Farm Diary" daily reels** — Anjula ji making products on the farm, not in a studio
2. **Ingredient origin stories** — "This mustard oil comes from our farm, see how we press it"
3. **Seasonal content** — mango pickle season (May-Jun), winter pickles (Nov-Dec)
4. **Behind-the-jar** — full journey from raw ingredient to sealed product
5. **Regional recipe battles** — "Rajasthani vs Punjabi mango pickle — which one wins?"
