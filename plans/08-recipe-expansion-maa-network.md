# Recipe Expansion — "Maa Ka Network" Strategy

> Post-Round 1: Sourcing authentic recipes from mothers across Indian states

---

## THE VISION

Like FarmDidi partners with rural women ("didis") across India, Mom Ka Nuska will build a **network of mothers** from different states — each contributing their region's signature recipes under the MKN brand.

**Phase 1 (Year 1):** Anjula ji's recipes only — Himachali + North Indian
**Phase 2 (Post-Round 1):** Add 2-3 regional mothers — new state-specific products
**Phase 3 (Year 3):** 10+ mothers across 8+ states — "India ki Maayein" collection

---

## HOW IT WORKS

### The Model

```
Mom Ka Nuska (Brand + Distribution + Content)
        │
        ├── Anjula Ji (Himachal) — Pickles, Chutneys, Snacks [CORE]
        │
        ├── Maa #2 (Rajasthan) — Ker Sangri, Lehsun Chutney, Mathri
        │
        ├── Maa #3 (Bengal) — Aam Kasundi, Tok Chutney, Mishti Mix
        │
        ├── Maa #4 (South India) — Avakaya, Gongura, Thokku
        │
        ├── Maa #5 (Gujarat) — Chhundo, Methia Masala, Fafda
        │
        └── Maa #6 (Bihar/Jharkhand) — Sattu Mix, Tilkut, Thekua
```

### For Each Mother

| Element | What We Do |
|---------|-----------|
| **Discovery** | Travel to state → find home cooks via local networks, YouTube, Instagram |
| **Validation** | Taste test, shelf-life test, production scalability check |
| **Documentation** | Record recipe SOP (video + written), standardize measurements |
| **Content** | Film "Meet [Name] Maa" series — her story, her kitchen, her state |
| **Production** | Options: (a) she produces from her home, we QC + pack, OR (b) she trains our team, we produce at Una farm |
| **Revenue share** | Fixed per-unit royalty (₹5-10/unit) + monthly retainer (₹10-15K) |
| **Brand** | Each product labeled: "Recipe by [Name] Maa, [State]" |

## CONTENT STRATEGY: "INDIA KI MAAYEIN"

### YouTube Series
- **Format:** 15-20 min documentary-style episodes
- **Each episode:** Travel to the state → meet the mother → learn her recipe → taste test → product reveal
- **Title pattern:** "EP 1: Rajasthan ki Maa — Ker Sangri ka Raaz"
- **Frequency:** 1 episode per new mother (monthly in Y2)

### Instagram Reels
- "Guess the state from the recipe" series
- Side-by-side: "Himachal ka achar vs Bengal ka achar"
- "Mom swap" — Anjula ji tries another state's recipe

### Brand Impact
- Every new mother = new audience from that state
- Regional pride → organic virality ("Our state's maa is on Mom Ka Nuska!")
- Each mother brings her own local network + word-of-mouth
- Content library grows exponentially

## DISCOVERY PROCESS

### How to Find Regional Mothers

1. **Instagram/YouTube search:** "Rajasthani cooking", "Bengali home recipe" → find home cooks with small followings (500-5K)
2. **Local food festivals:** Attend state-level food melas
3. **NGO partnerships:** Women's self-help groups (SHGs) already make pickles — partner with them
4. **Customer referrals:** Ask customers "Does your mom make something special? Tell us!"
5. **Local media:** Small-town newspaper features about women food entrepreneurs

### Selection Criteria

| Criteria | Must Have |
|----------|----------|
| Authenticity | Real home cook, not a commercial kitchen |
| Unique recipe | Something not available on Amazon/D2C brands |
| Story | Compelling personal journey (30+ years cooking, etc.) |
| Scalability | Recipe can be standardized + produced in batches |
| Shelf life | Product must last 3+ months without preservatives |
| Willingness | Comfortable on camera, willing to share recipe |
