## SLIDE 1: MAA KA NETWORK — THE VISION

### Prompt
```
Design a 16:9 slide titled "Phase 2: Maa Ka Network — India Ki Maayein". Show a map of India with dots on 10 states, each connected to a mother icon in the center (MKN brand). Each dot has state name + signature recipe. Center: MKN logo as the hub. Cream bg, terracotta dots, green lines.
```

### Data

**The Vision:**
Like FarmDidi partners with rural "didis," Mom Ka Nuska will build a network of mothers across India — each contributing their state's signature recipes.

- Phase 1 (Year 1): Anjula ji only — Himachali + North Indian
- Phase 2 (Post-Round 1): 2-3 regional mothers — new state-specific products
- Phase 3 (Year 3): 10+ mothers across 8+ states — "India Ki Maayein" collection

**The Model:**
```
Mom Ka Nuska (Brand + Distribution + Content)
        │
        ├── Anjula Ji (Himachal) — Pickles, Chutneys, Snacks [CORE]
        │
        ├── Maa #2 (Rajasthan) — Ker Sangri, Lehsun Chutney, Mathri
        │
        ├── Maa #3 (Bengal) — Aam Kasundi, Tok Chutney, Mishti Mix
        │
        ├── Maa #4 (Andhra/Telangana) — Avakaya, Gongura, Thokku
        │
        ├── Maa #5 (Gujarat) — Chhundo, Methia Masala, Fafda
        │
        └── Maa #6 (Bihar/Jharkhand) — Sattu Mix, Tilkut, Thekua
```

**What MKN Does for Each Mother:**
| Element | MKN's Role |
|---------|-----------|
| Discovery | Travel to state → find home cooks via local networks, YouTube, Instagram |
| Validation | Taste test, shelf-life test, production scalability check |
| Documentation | Record recipe SOP (video + written), standardize measurements |
| Content | Film "Meet [Name] Maa" series — her story, her kitchen, her state |
| Production | Option A: She produces, we QC + pack. Option B: She trains our team, we produce at Una |
| Revenue share | ₹5-10/unit royalty + ₹10-15K/month retainer |
| Brand | "Recipe by [Name] Maa, [State]" on every label |

## SLIDE 3: ECONOMICS PER MOTHER

### Prompt
```
Design a 16:9 slide titled "Economics — Cost Per Regional Mother". Show cost breakdown card on left. Revenue impact card on right. Bottom: scaling table (Y1 to Y3). Cream bg, green for ROI numbers.
```

### Data

**Cost Per Mother (Annual):**
| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Monthly retainer | ₹10-15K × 12 = ₹1.2-1.8L |
| Travel for filming | ₹15K (one-time) |
| Recipe development (3-5 products) | ₹10K (one-time) |
| New packaging/labels | ₹5K (one-time) |
| **Total Year 1 per mother** | **₹1.7-2.2L** |

**Revenue Impact Per Mother:**
| Metric | Conservative | Optimistic |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| New products added | 3-5 | 5-8 |
| Revenue from new SKUs/year | ₹15-25L | ₹30-50L |
| ROI on ₹2L investment | 7.5-12.5x | 15-25x |

**Scaling Impact:**
| Year | Mothers | New SKUs | Revenue from Network |
|------|---------|----------|---------------------|
| Y1 | 1 (Anjula only) | 15-20 | ₹1Cr (100% Anjula) |
| Y2 | 3-4 | 30-35 | ₹3-5Cr (60% Anjula, 40% network) |
| Y3 | 8-10 | 50+ | ₹25-50Cr (40% Anjula, 60% network) |

**How to Find Regional Mothers:**
1. Instagram/YouTube search: "Rajasthani cooking", "Bengali home recipe" → find home cooks (500-5K followers)
2. Local food festivals: Attend state-level food melas
3. NGO partnerships: Women's self-help groups (SHGs) already make pickles
4. Customer referrals: "Does YOUR mom make something special?"
5. Local media: Small-town newspaper features on women food entrepreneurs

**Selection Criteria:**
| Must Have | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| Real home cook (not commercial kitchen) | Authenticity |
| Unique recipe (not on Amazon/D2C) | Differentiation |
| Compelling personal story (30+ years) | Content |
| Recipe can be standardized for batches | Scalability |
| Product lasts 3+ months without preservatives | Shelf life |
| Comfortable on camera | Content |

**Legal:**
- MKN owns commercial rights (signed agreement)
- Permanent "Recipe by [Name] Maa" credit
- Exclusivity: cannot sell same recipe under competing brand
- 3-year renewable contract

## SLIDE 4: CONTENT STRATEGY — "INDIA KI MAAYEIN" SERIES

### Prompt
```
Design a 16:9 slide titled "Content: India Ki Maayein — YouTube Series". Show a YouTube-style video player with episode thumbnail. Below: series format, frequency, and brand impact metrics. Cream bg.
```

### Data

**YouTube Series: "India Ki Maayein"**
- Format: 15-20 min documentary-style episodes
- Each episode: Travel to state → meet the mother → learn her recipe → taste test → product reveal
- Title pattern: "EP 1: Rajasthan ki Maa — Ker Sangri ka Raaz"
- Frequency: 1 episode per new mother (monthly in Y2)
- Production cost: ₹15-25K per episode (travel + filming)

**Instagram Reels from Expansion:**
- "Guess the state from the recipe" series
- Side-by-side: "Himachal ka achar vs Bengal ka achar"
- "Mom swap" — Anjula ji tries another state's recipe
- Regional mother's daily Reels (filmed at their home)

**Brand Impact:**
| Metric | Before Expansion | After 3 Mothers |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Instagram reach | Himachal + North India | National (3 state audiences) |
| YouTube content | Farm + product | Documentary series |
| SKU count | 20 | 35+ |
| Audience diversity | Hindi-speaking urban | Multi-regional, multi-lingual |
| Organic traffic | 30-40% | 50-60% (each mother brings her local network) |
| Brand story depth | "One mother" | "India's mothers" — much more powerful |

**Key Insight:** Every new mother = new audience from that state. Regional pride drives organic virality: "Humari state ki maa Mom Ka Nuska pe hai!"
