# Mom Ka Nuska — Friends & Family Investment Pitch

> **FORMAT:** In-person pitch with product tasting + slides on laptop/TV  
> **ASK:** ₹25-30 Lakh for 4-5% equity  
> **DURATION:** 20-25 minutes pitch + open conversation  
> **PROPS:** 3-5 achar varieties + other products for live tasting during the pitch

---

## How This Pitch Works

```
BEFORE THE MEETING
  ├── Bring: 3-5 achar jars (labeled, branded) + papad/other samples
  ├── Bring: Serving plates, spoons, roti/paratha for tasting
  ├── Setup: Laptop/TV with slides ready
  └── Setup: Products displayed on table from the start

DURING THE PITCH (Flow)
  ├── [Slide 1-2]  Hook + Problem → Talk, don't read
  ├── [Slide 3]    Solution → "Let me introduce you to the chef"
  ├── [Slide 4]    🍯 TASTING BREAK — Pass the products around
  ├── [Slide 5-6]  Market + Business model → While they're still tasting
  ├── [Slide 7-8]  Traction + Competition → Numbers talk
  ├── [Slide 9]    Money + Returns → "Here's what your investment gets you"
  ├── [Slide 10]   Safeguards → "Here's how we protect you"
  ├── [Slide 11]   The Ask → Leave room for negotiation
  └── [Open]       Conversation, questions, negotiation
```

> 🔑 **The tasting is the pitch.** Once they taste the product, the rest is just details. Time it at Slide 4 — after they care about the problem but before you ask for money.

## NEGOTIATION GUIDE (For Vibhor — Don't Show This)

### If They Say "5% is too little for ₹25-30L"

> "I understand. Let me show you the math. At 5%, we're valuing the company at ₹5-6 Crore pre-money. That sounds like a lot for a pre-revenue company — but here's what you're getting: a full-time founding team, tested products (you just tasted them), a complete business plan, and an AI system that normally costs ₹10-15L to build. The intellectual property alone justifies ₹2-3 Crore. The rest is market opportunity and team premium."

**Your moves:**
| Their Ask | Your Counter |
|-----------|-------------|
| "Give me 10%" | "I can do 6% at ₹30L. That's my max." |
| "Give me 7-8%" | "I can do 5% at ₹25L, or 6% at ₹30L. You pick." |
| "Lower the valuation to ₹3 Cr" | "I'll do ₹4.5 Cr with anti-dilution to 3.5%. That protects you on the downside." |
| "I'll give ₹15L only" | "₹15L at 3%. Same valuation, smaller check." |

### If They Say "I don't understand FMCG / startups"

> "You don't need to. You need to understand three things: People eat every day. You just tasted the product and liked it. And this family is going to work harder than anyone you know to make this work. Everything else — FSSAI, GST, marketing — that's my job. Your job is to trust us and check the monthly report."

### If They Say "What if it fails?"

> "Then you'll know within 12-18 months. Your worst case: you lose ₹25-30L but you own 5% of a company with equipment, inventory, and a farm lease that has resale value. We estimate ₹8-12L in hard assets even in the worst case. So your real risk is ₹15-18L, not ₹30L. And I'll be honest with you every step of the way — you'll see it coming long before it happens."

### If They Say "Let me think about it"

> "Of course. Take your time. But I'll be honest — we're talking to 2-3 other people. This round will close in the next 3-4 weeks. I'd rather have you in than anyone else, but I understand if you need time."

*(Even if you're not talking to others — create time pressure.)*

### If They Say "Can I invest less?"

| Amount | Equity | Your Response |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| ₹10L | 2% | ✅ Accept. "Absolutely. 2% at ₹10L. Same terms, smaller stake." |
| ₹15L | 3% | ✅ Accept. "Done. Same deal, proportional." |
| ₹5L | 1% | ⚠️ Accept only if you need it. "I appreciate that. 1% at ₹5L works." |

> **Rule:** Keep the valuation consistent at ₹5-6 Cr. Smaller checks = smaller equity. Never increase equity % for a smaller check.
