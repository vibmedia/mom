# FMCG Market Intelligence

> Analyze markets, size opportunities, benchmark competitors, and identify strategic positioning for FMCG food brands.

## When to Use

- Sizing a new market opportunity (TAM/SAM/SOM)
- Running SWOT analysis for a brand or product
- Benchmarking against competitors
- Understanding regional/state-level food preferences
- Preparing market data for investor presentations
- Evaluating new product category entry

## Before Starting

1. **Which geography?** India national, state-specific, or international?
2. **Which product category?** (Pickles, masala, snacks, RTC kits, etc.)
3. **Purpose?** Investor prep, product launch, competitor defense, category entry?
4. **Existing data?** Any previous research, sales data, or industry reports?

## Core Framework

### TAM/SAM/SOM Analysis

| Level | Definition | How to Calculate |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| **TAM** (Total Addressable Market) | Entire market for the category in India | Industry reports (IBEF, Statista, IMARC) + Government data |
| **SAM** (Serviceable Addressable Market) | Portion you could realistically serve | Filter by: geography, channel (D2C), price segment, demographics |
| **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market) | Realistic first 3-year capture | Based on: marketing budget, production capacity, team size, competitor share |

### SWOT Framework (FMCG-specific)

| Factor | Internal Analysis | External Analysis |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Strengths** | What unique asset do you have? (recipe, story, farm, team) | What market trend supports you? |
| **Weaknesses** | What's missing? (scale, funding, distribution, brand awareness) | What barrier exists? |
| **Opportunities** | What can you build? (new SKUs, channels, regions) | What's growing? (D2C, health food, regional, NRI) |
| **Threats** | What could break? (single founder risk, cash flow) | What could hurt? (FMCG giants entering, regulation, price war) |

### Competitor Analysis Matrix

Analyze each competitor across these dimensions:

| Dimension | What to Capture |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Product Range** | Number of SKUs, categories, hero product |
| **Pricing** | Price range, avg SKU price, bundle offers |
| **Positioning** | Tag line, brand promise, emotional hook |
| **Channel Mix** | D2C %, marketplace %, retail %, B2B % |
| **Content Strategy** | Social followers, content type, frequency, engagement |
| **Packaging** | Quality, materials, design aesthetic, FSSAI compliance |
| **Reviews** | Amazon/Flipkart ratings, common complaints, praise points |
| **Funding** | Raised amount, investors, valuation (if public) |
| **Weakness** | What are customers complaining about? What's missing? |

### Regional Market Analysis

For any state/region, analyze:

| Factor | What to Capture |
|--------|----------------|
| **Population** | Total, urban %, internet penetration |
| **Food culture** | Signature flavors, cooking style, staple ingredients |
| **Local competitors** | Regional brands already serving this palate |
| **D2C readiness** | E-commerce adoption, delivery infra, payment preferences |
| **Festival calendar** | Peak buying seasons, gifting occasions |
| **Price sensitivity** | Willingness to pay for premium/authentic products |

## Common Mistakes

- ❌ Using outdated market data (always cite year)
- ❌ Inflating TAM to impress investors (VCs see through this)
- ❌ Ignoring regional competitors (they own local loyalty)
- ❌ Comparing D2C brands to FMCG giants (different playbook)
- ❌ Skipping the SOM — it's the only number that matters for execution
